About

This is a working archive of my academic work: notes, experiments, and things I'm thinking through.

I study physics, focused on complex systems. Nonlinear dynamics, chaos, networks, emergence. How simple rules create complicated behavior. I'm drawn to problems where abstract math connects to physical systems, whether through modeling, simulation, or geometric intuition.

You'll find course notes, explanations I wrote for myself, research explorations, and longer pieces where I'm working through ideas. Much of this is incomplete by design. These are ideas under construction, not polished conclusions. I use writing as a tool for thinking, not just communication.

My approach is systems-first. Less about isolated tricks, more about how parts interact, how information flows, and how collective behavior emerges across scales. This connects naturally to fluid dynamics, information theory, evolution, and network science.

This isn't a portfolio in the marketing sense. It's a public notebook meant to grow, break, and reorganize over time. If you're looking for finished answers, you may be disappointed. If you're interested in how a physicist thinks while learning, this is the right place.

About

This website is a working archive of my academic life and intellectual trajectory.

I'm a physics major with a primary interest in complex systems: nonlinear dynamics, chaos, networks, emergence, and how simple rules give rise to structured behavior. I'm especially drawn to problems where abstract mathematics can be grounded in physical systems‚Äîwhether through modeling, simulation, or geometric intuition.

The material here includes course notes, self-written explanations, research-oriented explorations, and long-form thought pieces. My approach to physics is systems-first. I care less about isolated tricks and more about understanding how parts interact, how information flows, and how collective behavior emerges across scales. This naturally intersects with areas like fluid dynamics, information theory, evolution, and network science.

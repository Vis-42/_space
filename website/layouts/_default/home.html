About

I'm a second-year physics student at NUS building toward research in complex systems and nonlinear dynamics. My work spans experimental physics (Hall effect, X-ray diffraction, magnetic moment measurements), computational modeling (quantum wavepacket simulations), and theoretical coursework. I'm seeking REU positions and research mentorship that combine theoretical rigor with computational and experimental approaches.

My focus is on how simple rules generate complex behavior across scales—nonlinear dynamics, chaos, networks, emergence. I'm particularly interested in problems where abstract mathematics connects to physical systems through modeling, simulation, or geometric intuition. This naturally intersects with fluid dynamics, information theory, and network science.

The work here includes analysis of uncertainty, methodological limitations, and where things went wrong—not just what worked. I use writing as a tool for thinking, so much of this is incomplete by design: ideas under construction rather than polished conclusions.

I work well with structured mentorship and clear feedback, and I'm looking for environments where I can contribute to ongoing projects while developing independent research direction.

Experimental Work

Hall Effect in Semiconductors

Goal: Determine carrier types and mobility in n-type and p-type Germanium; characterize temperature-dependent transition.

Methods: Hall voltage vs current/field measurements; magnetoresistance analysis; temperature sweeps (room temp to 143°C).

Findings: Cross-validated mobility through two independent methods (R² > 0.99); identified intrinsic transition at 106°C via sign reversal.

Skills demonstrated: Python data analysis, regression analysis, uncertainty propagation, theoretical validation.

X-Ray Diffraction Analysis

Goal: Determine LiF lattice constant; identify unknown crystal; extract Planck's constant from bremsstrahlung cutoff.

Methods: Bragg's law analysis; weighted peak averaging; bremsstrahlung cutoff extrapolation at multiple voltages.

Findings: LiF lattice constant within 2% accuracy; identified KBr (0.24% deviation); Planck's constant 31% off (S/N limited).

Skills demonstrated: Structural fingerprinting, error analysis, systematic error identification, cost-benefit analysis.

Magnetic Moment in Helmholtz Field

Goal: Calibrate Helmholtz field constant; validate electromagnetic torque scaling laws; determine unknown loop area.

Methods: Five independent experiments (current, turns, angle, diameter sweeps); multi-experiment calibration; residual analysis.

Findings: All scaling laws validated (R² > 0.997); Helmholtz constant 3.3% uncertainty; corrected diameter dependence (T ∝ d²).

Skills demonstrated: Cross-validation methodology, mathematical model correction, error budget construction, 3D visualization.

Electron Spin Resonance (ESR)

Goal: Extract g-factor of DPPH; validate ESR theory through frequency-field relationship.

Methods: Frequency-field sweeps (30-76.6 MHz); phase-sensitive detection with 50 Hz modulation.

Findings: g-factor extraction (R² = 0.9995); 4.9% deviation from free electron value; excellent linearity validates theory.

Skills demonstrated: Resonance condition analysis, Zeeman splitting interpretation, systematic error identification.

Propagation of Laser Light (PLL)

Goal: Characterize Gaussian beam propagation; validate Gaussian beam optics model.

Methods: Transverse intensity scans at 8 distances (10 cm to 15 m); Gaussian profile fitting.

Findings: Beam waist and Rayleigh range with sub-1% agreement to theory; M² = 1.08 (near-ideal Gaussian).

Skills demonstrated: Beam parameter extraction, theoretical validation, uncertainty analysis, spatial resolution optimization.

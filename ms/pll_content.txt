PHYSICS IMPORTANCE:

WHY THIS MATTERS:
Gaussian beams maintain their shape during propagation - making them predictable and controllable for precision applications. This experiment validates the mathematical foundation underlying all laser technology.

=== EXPERIMENTAL OBJECTIVES & CHALLENGE ===

PRIMARY GOALS:
1. Measure beam waist W₀ of He-Ne laser (λ = 632.8 nm)
2. Determine Rayleigh range Z_R from beam evolution
3. Calculate beam quality M² to assess Gaussian behavior
4. Verify theoretical predictions of Gaussian beam optics

KEY CHALLENGE:
Precise intensity measurements across 150× distance range (10 cm → 15 m while maintaining <2% uncertainty)

=== MATHEMATICAL FOUNDATION ===

GAUSSIAN INTENSITY:
I(r,z) = I₀ exp(-2r²/W²(z))

BEAM EVOLUTION:
W(z) = W₀ √(1 + (z/Z_R)²)

RAYLEIGH RANGE:
Z_R = πW₀²/λ

BEAM QUALITY:
M² = Θπw₀/λ
Where Θ = experimental divergence

PHYSICAL MEANING: These equations describe how diffraction causes beam spreading while maintaining Gaussian shape

=== EXPERIMENTAL SETUP & METHOD ===

EQUIPMENT:
- He-Ne laser (632.8 nm, 5 mW)
- Photodetector on micrometer stage
- Digital multimeter for voltage
- Variable attenuator (prevent saturation)

MEASUREMENT PROTOCOL:
- 8 positions: 10 cm → 15 m
- Horizontal scans: 10-20 points each
- Vertical optimization at every distance

Linear arrangement: Laser → Attenuator → Detector

=== RAW DATA: GAUSSIAN PROFILES ===

KEY OBSERVATIONS:
✓ Perfect Gaussian profiles at all distances
✓ Systematic beam spreading with distance
✓ Maintained symmetry about beam axis
✓ Peak intensity decrease due to expansion

FITTING FUNCTION:
I(r) = I₀ exp(-2r²/W²)
R² > 0.998 for all fits

=== EXTRACTED PARAMETERS & EVOLUTION ===

EXTRACTED PARAMETERS:
- Beam waist: W₀ = (0.394 ± 0.008) mm
- Rayleigh range: Z_R = (770 ± 15) mm
- Waist location: z₀ = (295 ± 10) mm from reference
